Gen Z has a general tendency to label each summer through a song and/or album that dominated the charts. Last year was one of the best for pop music in a while; the song “Espresso” by Sabrina Carpenter and the album “Brat” by Charli xcx are emblematic of that time. The cultural impact of “Brat” in particular led to the summer of 2024 being unanimously dubbed “Brat Summer.”

Expectations for the music of 2025 ran high after such an iconic year of hits, which led to relative uproar over the lack of a ‘song of the summer’ this year. Yes, songs like “Ordinary” by Alex Warren, “What I Want” by Morgan Wal- len featuring Tate McRae, and “Golden” from the soundtrack of the Netflix movie “KPop Demon Hunters”, were undoubtedly extremely popular. However, they have lacked the impact needed to define the whole season. As a result, many people, including myself, have picked their own songs of the summer that defined their life during that period. Here are my candidates:

McKinley Dixon released his newest studio album “Magic, Alive!” on Jun. 6. This jazz rap album features mesmerizing jazz arrangements backing Dixon’s voice to create one of the most memorable musical experiences I have had in 2025. The tracks “Sugar Water”, featuring Quelle Chris and Anjimile, and “Could’ve Been Different”, featuring Blu and Shamir, are two potent examples of the album’s identity and what makes it so special.

Another striking musical project was London-based artist Pink Pantheress’ mixtape “Fancy That”. After its release on May 9, the collection of tracks displays Pink Pantheress’ evolved musical identity and her general skills in music production. I have been a longtime fan of Pink Pantheress and found “Fancy That” to be my favorite project of hers to date. Although it is a short listen of just over 20 minutes, the mixtape shares the fun that she had producing it with the listener. My favorite tracks were “Illegal” and “Romeo” because they stick out in both identity and the infectious amusement “Fancy That” contains so much of.

Photo courtesy of Apple Music

The album cover of Fancy That by PinkPantheress.

While the summer 2025 may have been unremarkable to many listeners, it was still a fruitful period for music. “Magic, Alive!” and “Fancy That” captured my summer, but there have been other recent releases that I have found particularly noteworthy. “Sincerely” by Kali Uchis, “Eusexua” by FKA Twigs, and “Sinister Gift” by Panda Bear are just a few more of the many high quality albums from this year that I recommend exploring.
