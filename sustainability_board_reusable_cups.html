Sustainability board's new reusable cup initiative

One of the many cups that have been raffled off this week by the Sustainability Board, featuring their logo on a premium travel coffee mug.

Grace Muliero '28

September 17, 2025

Volume LVI, Issue #1

As the first few weeks of the new school year begin to settle in, a new Wheaton Sustainability Board initiative has been launched: reusable cups that save students money when used at the on-campus Starbucks were up for raffle. In an event spanning from Aug. 31 to Sept. 13, 100 new Sustainability Board-branded cups have been raffled off to some lucky Wheaton students. The raffle, which was available to enter online, selected winners using a wheel. Each cup can be used at dining locations around campus, but when used in person at the Starbucks attached to the 1834 Grill, the cup can save the user 25 cents on any drink made and served in the cup.

When asked via email about the new initiative, the Sustainability Board’s Vice Chair, Jill Imrie ‘27, said, “We are encouraging sustainability and rejecting consumerism with this project.” Imrie continued stating that the Sustainability Board’s “ultimate goal is to eventually work with the college to give out these cups, or something similar, to all incoming freshmen. This would create an environment where sustainable practices are everyday practices for our Wheaton community.”

While the raffle for these cups has concluded, the Sustainability Board has numerous upcoming initiatives that promote sustainability. Past events include distributing sustainable period products, campus cleanups, pollinator picnics, and the semiannual clothing swap with Ecco House. To stay up-to-date on these events and future sustainability initiatives, follow the board on Instagram @wheatonsustainability or join their page on Engage to receive email reminders of meetings and events.
