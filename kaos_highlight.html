You may know K-pop through several mediums, including the new hit animated film “KPop Demon Hunters”. The film features several well-known celebrities as well as real-life K-pop idols and singers. You also have probably heard songs from famous bands such as BTS, Seventeen, or KATSEYE, who debuted with record success last June.

KAOS aims to capture that excitement of Korean culture and uses it to perform in several of Wheaton’s dance events, including WheaPerform and DanceFest. The team also performs covers of popular songs, most recently NCT U’s “Make a Wish”, and uploads them to the group’s Youtube channel called KAOS Dance. Some of the group’s favorite past performances have come from WheaP- erform and DanceFest, with most of the exec board citing “Gangnam Style” by PSY as a fan and team-favorite.

WheaPerform is one of the most important times of the year for the group, as a strong first showing encourages first-years and other students to audition for KAOS. Returning members range from the class of 2026 to the class 2028, and are hoping to recruit both members from the sophomore class of 2028 and the freshman class of 2029. When speaking with KAOS, members Charlotte Varga ’26 (President), Keandre Alexander ’27 (Dance Captain), and Heidi Ricker ’28 (Dance Captain) emphasized the importance of having fun, being inclusive, and creating an open space to help facilitate practices and performances.

The team strongly encourages trying out even if you don’t know much K-pop but want to join a fun dance group. KAOS has a no-experience-needed policy, meaning you can audition regardless of how much K-pop knowledge and dancing skill you have.

Currently practicing three times a week, the team works on their routines for both recorded covers and live dance events. Costumes, which can range from elaborate to chic, and comfy outfits are key parts of their presentation.

The group is actively planning their fall semester schedule, and is exploring potential collaborations with other Wheaton College dance teams.

Be sure to check out both their Youtube and Instagram (@kaosdance) accounts for updates on future content, shows and events!
