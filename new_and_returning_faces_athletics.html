Volume LVI, Issue #1

Wheaton Athletics saw a big change over the summer with two new coaches hired, three coaches promoted, and two changes in administrative roles. New to Whea ton are Dan Tischler, the head baseball coach, and Lindsey Keen, the artistic swimming coach. Both have extensive experience in their sports and have set goals for both this season and future years.

Tischler previously was at Boston College as an assistant coach for 2 seasons and spent 11 seasons prior to that at Columbia University as an assistant coach and associate head coach. He said being at Wheaton brings “more responsibility for every facet of the program than I have ever had. From organizing practice, to alumni relations, to secondary duties within the department, there’s just more. It is an exciting opportunity.” He plans for this year’s team to be “one of the most competitive in our conference,” one that other schools are scared to play.

In the administrative office, Lisa Yenush '90 took on the title of Interim Senior Director of Athletics after previously being Associate Director. While the role is temporary in nature, she is very excited about all the recent changes, from new coaches to new programs. Yenush was both a student athlete and resident advisor during her time at Wheaton as a student. "My job is to support (others in athletics) as best as I can," she said. This fall marks the beginning of her 13th year at Wheaton and she is "oozing with pride" for the school.

Keen joins the artisic swimming team from the Pacific Wabes club artistic swimming team in Seattle, after being approached for the position by USA Artistic Swimming CEO Adam Andrasko. Keen sees this as a new opportunity, as the collegiate system has various intricacies that the club system does not. She noted that, "I had the support of USA Artistic Swimming, which gave me the confidence to pursue this." Additionally, this new role offers her the ability to work with higher level, more intense athletes. Keen mentioned that Wheaton "felt like home," during her interviews with the athletics team and athletes. In her first season with Wheaton she is hoping to get the team "back on the map as a real contender for top three," and to "be a threat again."

Brian Walmsley, the previous head coach of men's basketball for 28 years, is now the Senior Associate Director for Athletics. Yenush noted how Walmsley's transition was seamless and how he has everyone's trust. Alex DuBrow '20 takes the place of Walmsley as the Interim Head Coach, an advancement from his prior position as Assistant Men's Basketball Coach for the past two years. He noted that the role is similar, but with more responsibility. "I was fortunate that Coach Walmsley gave me a lot of responsibility within all aspects of the program, so stepping into this new role I feel comfortable," he commented. DuBrow competed on the men's basketball team as a student before graduating from Wheaton in 2020.

Alexander Evans and Luis Platas have also been promoted from their past positions to head coaches. Evans was just formally named the head coach for both the women and men's cross country teams as he enters his sixth season at Wheaton. Platas is now the head coach for the men's tennis program, with Dan Munsey becoming the Director of Tennis but continuing his role as head coach for the women's program. More information on these promotions can be found at wheatoncollegelyons.com.
