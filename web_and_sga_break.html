WEB and SGA: A fall semester break-up

Deren Sozer ‘27

September 17, 2025

Volume LVI, Issue #1

Wheaton Events Board is saying goodbye to student government. In a special meeting on Sept. 2, Wheaton’s Student Government Association’s (SGA) Senate voted to approve a proposal which would remove Wheaton Events Board (WEB) as an SGA Executive Board.

The proposal “recommends transitioning the Wheaton Events Board (WEB) from an official Board of the Student Government Association (SGA) to an independent, student-run organization.” It contends that the move would make WEB more flexible and autonomous.

A number of reasons were given for the change. Chief among these are the “bureaucratic constraints” the original structure placed on WEB, as well as the difference between both organizations’ missions. “As part of SGA, WEB often experiences role confusion,” the proposal states, “both internally and in how it is perceived by students.”

“A big part of the proposal was showing the difference between the identity of SGA and WEB’s purpose,” said Ariel Ferreira, assistant director of student engagement and programs. A large part of Ferreira’s job is advising WEB and overseeing their event planning and budget.

“WEB is the event planning board of Wheaton College, by students, and SGA is really based on advocacy,” she said, “and their purpose is to advocate and make change in that way, where WEB is supposed to be focused on making impactful events for the whole campus.”

Aside from the compatibility issues between the organizations, WEB was experiencing issues filling its president’s position due to the high commitment requirement of SGA. “We realized we were seeing burnout in the president’s role, and also the types of folks that normally want to be president of an events board aren’t really the type that would want to run for office,” she said. “We’ve seen consistently over the past two or three years, nobody runs for the position, they get written in, and then they feel like they have to accept it.”

In this past cycle, no one accepted the role. According to Ferreira, the lack of a president hampered WEB’s ability to plan events as usual and prompted an evaluation of the organization’s structure. The process began with speaking with returning WEB members and SGA’s Core Four, and from there, co-writing the proposal.

“It’s kind of impressive that it was pulled off the way it was,” Ferreira said, “because everyone was in great communication and, moreover, knew the facts and what they were talking about.”

Going forward, WEB will still have a relationship with SGA, as Ferreira stressed. “We’re not actually breaking away from SGA, it’s just going through different avenues.”

No longer an executive board, however, WEB’s president will no longer be required to attend SGA Senate and Executive Board meetings, freeing up significant time in their schedule, according to the proposal’s proponents. There are plans for WEB to send a representative to Senate to provide updates.

SGA Vice President Sara Milesi led the meeting in which the proposal was debated and was involved in the process. “SGA will still allocate funds to WEB and support their group, just in a different way,” she said. “WEB drafted this proposal with the help of SAIL (Student Activities, Involvement, and Leadership), who will be better equipped to provide resources to WEB.”

According to the proposal, budgets for WEB “will be submitted and reviewed through the same processes as other clubs and organizations.”
